Oyster omelette

A hawker is making oyster omelette in the Shilin Night Market of Taipei.The oyster omelette (Chinese: ???; Pe?h-oe-ji: ô-á-chian) is a dish of Teochew origin that is widely known for its savoury taste in its native Chaoshan along with Taiwan, Fujian, and many parts of Southeast Asia due to the influence of the Chinese diaspora. Variations of the dish preside in some southern regions of China, although the taste and appearance of these can vary by a lot from the Taiwanese version. The oyster omelette is a Taiwanese "night market favorite", and has constantly been ranked by many foreigners as the top dish from Taiwan. Its generous proportions and affordable price demonstrates the trait of night market cuisines. It is also popular in other places with Chaoshan and Fujianese influences, such as in Hong Kong, Macau, Singapore, Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia and Philippines.For Bangkok in Thailand, notable areas in oyster omelette include Talat Wang Lang near Siriraj Hospital and Wang Lang (Siriraj) Pier in Bangkok Noi there are two restaurants and Yaowarat neighborhood, which there is one Michelin-Bib Gourmand restaurant with Charoen Krung neighborhood in Bang Rak etc.In 2017, the World Street Food Congress was announced that oyster omelette is one of the three most notable street food among the street foods of Thailand.

Douhua

Douhua (Chinese: ??; pinyin: dòuhua) is the short form of doufuhua (Chinese: ???; pinyin: dòufuhua). It is a Chinese snack made with very soft tofu. It is also referred to as tofu pudding and soybean pudding.But Zhangzhou's Douhua is different than other,it is cooking with cellophane noodles.You can put Lou mei in it!

Platostoma palustre jelly

It is made from Platostoma palustre and is one of the famous snacks in Fujian. There are two ways to eat it. One of them is also popular in Guangdong, Hong Kong and Macao, but it is called jelly in these areas.You can add milk and chopped peanut.
